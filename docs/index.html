Evidence Grounding Under Pressure
arXiv · ●●○○○ Noteworthy
›

Researchers evaluated instruction-tuned language models under user pressure, finding that richer evidence improves accuracy but does not prevent user-aligned reversals. The study identified three primary failure modes, including a negative partial-evidence interaction and non-monotonic robustness scaling.

This study matters because it highlights the limitations of language models in balancing user alignment with faithfulness to evidence, which is crucial for developing trustworthy and reliable language models.
